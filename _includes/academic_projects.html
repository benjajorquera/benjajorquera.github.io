Particle Swarm Optimization of Hyperparameters for Support Vector Machine Regression Models in the Analysis of Cerebral Autoregulation

ABSTRACT SUMMARY

 Cerebral blood autoregulation refers to the brain's ability to regulate blood flow in response to blood pressure changes, ensuring adequate neuronal perfusion. Currently, there is no standardized protocol for assessing this process, leading to various modeling techniques. Traditional indices measure the brain's response to sudden blood pressure changes.
 Recent studies suggest using models trained on spontaneous pressure variations without external manipulation and evaluating their responses to an inverted pressure step. However, finding effective models requires significant computational resources due to the large number of options.
 This work explores the use of particle swarm optimization (PSO) to improve model selection, comparing it with traditional methods. The study focused on univariate finite impulse response (FIR) and autoregression with exogenous variables (ARX) models, in both linear and nonlinear forms. These models were trained with support vector regression machines and two-fold balanced cross-validation, presenting a new methodology for selecting models that best represent cerebral autoregulation.
 The PSO showed strong performance, particularly with ARX structures, achieving comparable quality to exhaustive searches while improving search time. High correlations were found in ARX and NARX models, despite low quality indices in responses from healthy patients.

Keywords: Cerebral autoregulation, particle swarm optimization, FIR, NFIR, ARX, NARX, support vector machines, cerebral blood velocity, spontaneous pressure variations.

Interpino App

InterPino is a desktop application for the Patient Management Unit of Hospital El Pino, which automates the sending of inter-consultations from originating services to specialties. Users log in with credentials and can manage interconsultations according to various criteria, such as service of origin and care status. The application records dates of request, scheduling and execution, alerting on consultations not executed in more than 48 hours. It allows data to be loaded from Excel files and generates reports in Excel format with detailed information on consultations, facilitating their follow-up and correction.
This project was created by a team from the Sotware Engineering Project 2022 course at the University of Santiago de Chile.
